-- The countdown to Christmas has officially started and that can only mean one thing - it's time for the big shops to try and win us over with their heartwarming and tear jerking Christmas adverts.
This year Marks and Spencer was one of the first to release their TV ad, which sees Paddington Bear become an unlikely hero.
The famous bear saves Christmas as he unwittingly stops a hapless burglar and delivers all the stolen presents back to the good girls and boys.
However some aren't as impressed, and it's mainly because of one little detail.
Throughout the advert Paddington Bear calls his red hooded friend Santa, and at the end when he's feeling a bit more formal, Mr Claus.
However viewers claim that Paddington, who lives with the Brown family in London, should really be calling him Father Christmas.
One wrote: "Lovely advert but I am pretty sure that Paddington, living with the Brown family in London, as he does would refer to the chap in red as Father Christmas!"
Another viewer agreed, saying: "The browns would of definitely called the big man Father Christmas."
One Twitter user wrote: "Love it apart from the fact it's FATHER CHRISTMAS NOT SANTA. We are English."
Another added: "Please M&S ...it's 'Father Christmas'. You are a British retailer. #disappointed."
However many people immediately jumped to the advert's defence.
Most people are completely in love with the adorable advert , with many claiming it's the company's best ever.
